## What Is an N-of-1 Study and Why Use It for NAD+ Testing?

An N-of-1 study is a clinical trial where you are both the researcher and the single subject. Unlike population studies that average results across thousands of participants, N-of-1 studies focus on determining what works specifically for your unique physiology, lifestyle, and health status.

For NAD+ supplements, this approach is particularly valuable because:

- **Individual variation is massive**: NAD+ metabolism varies significantly based on age, genetics, stress levels, and existing mitochondrial health
- **Expensive interventions deserve rigorous testing**: At $100-300/month, you need confidence in measurable benefits
- **Placebo effects are strong**: Energy and cognitive improvements are subjective and susceptible to expectation bias
- **Timing matters**: NAD+ effects may take weeks to manifest and could vary seasonally

Traditional supplement tracking apps like MyFitnessPal or Heads Up Health can correlate your NAD+ intake with basic metrics, but they lack the statistical rigor needed to establish causation. Consumer platforms like SelfDecode provide population-based recommendations but can't tell you if NAD+ specifically improves *your* energy levels versus other interventions.

## Can You Do a Research Study on Your Own? The Methodology Challenge

Yes, you absolutely can conduct rigorous research on yourself—but most biohackers make critical methodology mistakes that invalidate their results. Common errors include:

- **No baseline period**: Starting NAD+ immediately without establishing pre-intervention metrics
- **Confounding variables**: Changing diet, exercise, or sleep habits simultaneously
- **Insufficient duration**: Testing for only 2-3 weeks when NAD+ effects may take 6-8 weeks
- **No washout periods**: Never cycling off to confirm the supplement is causing observed benefits
- **Poor outcome measurement**: Relying on subjective "feeling better" without validated instruments

## NAD+ N-of-1 Protocol: 16-Week Structured Testing Framework

### Phase 1: Baseline Establishment (Weeks 1-4)

**Objective**: Establish stable baseline measurements across all outcome domains before intervention.

**Daily Measurements**:
- **Energy levels**: Fatigue Assessment Scale (FAS) - validated 10-item questionnaire, scored 1-5 daily
- **Sleep quality**: Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) weekly + objective sleep tracking (Oura, WHOOP, or Garmin)
- **Cognitive performance**: Cambridge Brain Training app or Lumosity - 3 specific games daily, same time
- **Physical performance**: Resting heart rate, heart rate variability (HRV), grip strength (2x/week)

**Weekly Measurements**:
- **Subjective wellbeing**: WHO-5 Well-Being Index
- **Stress levels**: Perceived Stress Scale (PSS-4)
- **Body composition**: Weight, body fat % (DEXA if accessible, bioimpedance otherwise)

**Lifestyle Controls**:
- Maintain current supplement stack (no changes)
- Document alcohol consumption, caffeine intake, major stressors
- Keep consistent sleep/wake times (±30 minutes)
- Maintain regular exercise routine

### Phase 2: First Intervention Period (Weeks 5-10)

**NAD+ Protocol**:
- Choose your NAD+ precursor (NMN 250-500mg or NR 300-600mg daily)
- Take with morning meal for consistent absorption
- Source from reputable manufacturer with third-party testing
- Document exact product, batch number, dosage timing

**Measurement Continuity**:
- Continue all baseline measurements without changes
- Add supplement-specific tracking: morning energy rating (1-10), afternoon energy crash assessment
- Weekly check-ins: rate overall energy, sleep, focus on 1-10 scales

**Critical Controls**:
- Change ONLY the NAD+ addition—no other supplement, diet, or lifestyle modifications
- If you get sick or experience major life stress, note it but continue measurements
- Use pill reminder app to ensure consistent timing and dosing

### Phase 3: Washout Period (Weeks 11-12)

**Protocol**: Stop NAD+ supplementation completely while maintaining all measurements.

**Rationale**: This washout period is crucial for establishing causation. If benefits were truly from NAD+, you should see regression toward baseline levels.

**Key Observations**:
- How quickly do energy levels change?
- Do sleep improvements persist or decline?
- Are cognitive performance gains maintained?

### Phase 4: Second Intervention (Weeks 13-16)

**Re-introduction**: Resume NAD+ at the same dose and timing as Phase 2.

**Enhanced Tracking**: Since you now have baseline and first intervention data, focus on replication:
- Do the same improvements return?
- How quickly do benefits re-emerge?
- Are effects stronger, weaker, or consistent with Phase 2?

## Single Subject Research Design: Statistical Analysis for NAD+ Effects

### Effect Size Calculation

For energy improvements to be clinically meaningful (not just placebo effect), you need:
- **Fatigue Assessment Scale**: ≥3-point improvement from baseline average
- **Cognitive performance**: ≥10% improvement in reaction time or accuracy on consistent tasks
- **Sleep quality**: ≥1-point improvement in PSQI global score
- **HRV improvements**: ≥10ms increase in RMSSD from baseline average

### Data Analysis Framework

**Trend Analysis**: Plot your weekly averages across all phases. Look for:
- Clear improvement during intervention phases
- Regression during washout period
- Consistent replication in second intervention

**Statistical Significance**: Use two-standard-deviation rule—improvements should exceed 2x the standard deviation of your baseline measurements to be considered real effects versus normal variation.

**Confounding Variable Control**: If major life changes occur (illness, travel, stress), use statistical techniques to adjust for these factors or consider extending the study duration.

## Avoiding Common N-of-1 Study Mistakes

**Insufficient Statistical Power**: Single-subject studies require longer durations than population studies. Plan for minimum 16-week protocols for supplements with gradual effects like NAD+ precursors.

**Outcome Measure Selection**: Choose validated clinical instruments over subjective ratings. "I feel more energetic" is less reliable than standardized fatigue assessment scales used in clinical research.

**Confounding Variable Management**: The biggest threat to validity is changing multiple variables simultaneously. Resist the urge to optimize diet, exercise, and supplements concurrently.

**Publication Bias in Personal Experiments**: You're more likely to continue measuring when you perceive benefits and stop when you don't. Commit to completing the full protocol regardless of early impressions.
